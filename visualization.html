Visualization of missingness
Visualizing missingness is important for understanding the patterns of missing values in a dataset. Interactive visualization techniques, such as parallel coordinates and matrix plots, can be used to effectively identify and explore patterns of missingness in a dataset. These techniques can be used to clearly visualize the distribution of missing values across variables, as well as to identify variables with higher levels of missingness. Furthermore, interactive features can be used to allow users to dynamically explore and compare the patterns of missingness across different subsets of the data. By making use of interactive visualization techniques, one can gain insight into the patterns of missingness that can inform decision-making about the data.
